♛ How to Play Five Crowns

Starting the Game

Choose a dealer. The dealer shuffles both decks together and deals 3 cards to each player for Round 1. Each subsequent round, one more card is dealt — so Round 2 deals 4, Round 3 deals 5, up to Round 11 (Kings) which deals 13. Play begins with the player to the dealer's left and goes clockwise. The dealer role passes left each round.

Taking a Turn

On your turn: draw one card from either the top of the draw pile or the top of the discard pile. Then discard one card. You always end your turn with the same number of cards you were dealt.

Books & Runs

  • Book: 3 or more cards of the same value (any suits)
  • Run: 3 or more consecutive cards of the same suit
  • Wild cards and Jokers can sub for any card in a book or run
  • You cannot play on another player's books or runs

Going Out

When your entire hand can be arranged into books and/or runs, you may go out: lay down all your cards in books and/or runs — using exactly the number of cards you were dealt — then discard your last card. After someone goes out, every other player gets one more turn to improve their hand before scoring. Any cards that cannot be played into a book or run will count against the player's score.

Wild Cards

The wild card changes each round based on how many cards are dealt: Round 1 deals 3 cards, so 3s are wild. Round 2 deals 4 cards, so 4s are wild. This continues through Round 11, which deals 13 cards and makes Kings wild. Jokers are always wild in every round.

Scoring

  • All cards played in completed books or runs do not count against your score
  • Remaining cards count against you:
  • Number cards = face value  |  J = 11  |  Q = 12  |  K = 13
  • Wild cards (the round's wild) = 20 pts each
  • Jokers = 50 pts each

After 11 rounds, the player with the lowest total score wins. In a tie, players share the victory or play a 6-card tiebreaker.
