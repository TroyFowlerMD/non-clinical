About hydration

Hydration is the added water as a percentage of your starting flour. Lower hydration, around 60-65%, usually feels firmer, easier to shape, and more forgiving. Higher hydration, around 75-85%, can produce a more open crumb but is stickier and asks for gentler handling.

For flour volume, this calculator assumes bread flour at about 125 g per US cup. Scooping style and flour brand can move that number, so grams remain the target.

About starter amount

Starter percentage is inoculation: how much active starter you add compared with the starting flour. A common working range is 10-30%. More starter usually speeds fermentation; less starter usually slows it down.

This v1 assumes a 100% hydration starter, so starter contributes both flour and water. That raises the effective total hydration compared with the base hydration setting.

About salt

Salt is typically about 2% of starting flour. It improves flavor and dough strength, and it can slow fermentation somewhat.

Spoon estimates vary by crystal shape: table salt packs more densely than kosher salts. Use the grams as the target and the teaspoon estimate as a backup kitchen guide.

Advanced math

Starter is assumed to be 100% hydration, so half of its weight counts as flour and half counts as water. Effective hydration includes those starter contributions.

1. Dough Prep & Bulk

  • Mix to shaggy dough
  • Rest 30-60 min
  • 4-5 fold sets over first 2 hrs
  • Bulk until ~50% rise (1-4 hrs)

Mix & Autolyse 30-60 minutes

Combine water + active starter + flour + salt, then mix to shaggy dough.
Cover in a bowl and rest 30-60 min.

Bulk Fermentation about 3-6 hours total

Transfer to a clean, lightly oiled container.
Do 4-5 sets of stretch-and-folds over first 2 hours.
Rest 30 min between each set.
After last set, cover and ferment until ~50% volume increase (1-4 hours, temp-dependent).

2. Shape & Final Proof

  • Shape tight
  • Choose room or cold proof
  • Room proof: 1-3 hrs
  • Cold proof: 12-24 hrs

Shape

Turn dough onto lightly floured surface.
Shape into tight ball or loaf, creating surface tension.

Final Proof choose one

Room temp: in banneton, seam up; proof 1-3 hours.
Cold proof (preferred): in banneton, seam up; cover and refrigerate 12-24 hours.

3. Bake & Cool

  • Preheat Dutch oven
  • Bake covered, then uncovered
  • Covered: 20-30 min
  • Uncovered: 10-15 min
  • Cool at least 1 hr

Preheat

Dutch oven in oven.
Preheat to 450-550°F (230-290°C) for 30-60 min.

Score & Bake

Turn dough onto parchment, smooth side up.
Score with razor.
Bake in covered Dutch oven 20-30 min.
Uncover and bake additional 10-15 min, until crust is deeply browned and crisp.

Cool

Remove loaf to wire rack.
Cool at least 1 hour before slicing.
