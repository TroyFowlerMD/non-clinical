POA Logistics Checklist What daughter needs to do, and when

Lewis County, Washington

Get the POA set up in one visit.

This is a practical checklist for the daughter. Assume mother cannot do the legwork because of limited mobility. Daughter should prepare, print, schedule, drive, call, scan, mail or upload, and follow up.

Do these first. They take the most time.

  1. Print all forms before the visit.
  2. Schedule a notary or bank notary appointment.
  3. Gather IDs, benefit cards, bank details, pension papers, tax records, and hospital bills.
  4. Call the bank to ask what POA agent paperwork they require.
  5. Call pension/union administrator with mother present or ready to join.
  6. Scan every signed page to PDF before the day ends.

Before leaving the house

Daughter should gather these. If something is missing, write down where to get it and keep moving.

First stepPrint or save

Forms to have ready

  • Washington Durable Financial POA and Health Care POA from Washington Law Help.
  • DSHS Authorized Representative Form 14-532.
  • IRS Form 2848 and/or IRS Form 8821.
  • Washington Department of Revenue Confidential Tax Information Authorization, only if a Washington state tax, business, estate, or property tax issue applies.
  • HIPAA or medical release forms from each hospital or clinic, if available.
  • Bank POA certification or agent forms, if the bank requires its own form.
  • Pension or union authorization form, if the plan provides one in advance.
Bring to appointment

Information to gather

  • Mother's and daughter's government photo IDs.
  • Social Security numbers for both.
  • Medicare card or 65th birthday timing; Medicaid/Apple Health card or case number; insurance cards.
  • Bank names, account numbers, routing numbers, recurring bills, and hospital account numbers.
  • Pension payment stubs, 1099-R forms, divorce decree or property settlement, QDRO or pension letters.
  • Prior tax returns, IRS notices, W-2s, 1099s, and Social Security statements.

Must do while daughter is physically with mother

These are the high-value tasks for the one-day visit because they require mother's signature, ID, voice authorization, or physical presence.

Highest priorityTogether

1. Notarize the core documents

Use a mobile notary, bank notary, UPS Store notary, or valid Washington remote online notary. Call ahead.

  • Mother signs Washington Durable Financial POA.
  • Mother signs Washington Health Care POA.
  • Mother signs HIPAA/medical release authorization.
  • Mother and daughter sign DSHS 14-532.
  • Mother signs IRS 2848 and/or 8821.
  • Mother signs Washington DOR tax authorization if a state tax issue applies.
  • Mother signs bank or pension forms if obtained in advance.

Do not skipTogether

2. Visit the bank

Ask to add daughter as agent under Power of Attorney, not joint owner. Joint ownership can create Medicaid, tax, and estate problems.

  • Bring notarized durable financial POA and both IDs.
  • Ask for the bank's internal POA certification or agent form.
  • Ask for online/mobile banking access for the agent.
  • Ask for bill pay setup and statement access.
  • Get the direct phone number and remote follow-up procedure.
High priorityTogether if possible

3. DSHS / Apple Health Medicaid

Submit DSHS 14-532 and the POA, or at least call with mother present. Ask daughter to receive notices, renewals, case information, and MCO details.

  • Submit DSHS 14-532.
  • Provide a copy of the notarized durable financial POA.
  • Ask about long-term care Medicaid if in-home care or nursing care could be needed.
  • Update mailing address, phone, email preferences, and managed care plan information.

Time-sensitiveMother present by phone

4. Pension / union administrator

Call with mother present because ERISA pension plans may require their own authorization even when a state POA exists.

  • Confirm exact plan name, administrator, and participant ID.
  • Ask whether the Washington Durable Financial POA is accepted.
  • Request plan-specific authorization if needed.
  • Ask for missing 1099-R forms and confirm benefit amount, payment schedule, and direct deposit.
  • Ask whether the divorce pension division order/QDRO is on file.
Possible pension contacts

Check stubs or 1099-R first. Darigold workers in Washington have been represented by Teamsters Local 117, and the plan may be Western Conference of Teamsters Pension Trust or a related dairy industry plan.

  • Western Conference of Teamsters Pension Trust
  • ASP Benefits, dairy industry plan administration
Together preferred

5. Hospitals and clinics

If visiting in person is possible, sign release and proxy access forms while mother can sign. If not, daughter can call later and ask how to submit forms by fax, portal, or mail.

  • Sign HIPAA/release of information forms.
  • Sign patient portal proxy or representative access forms.
  • Sign billing/financial assistance authorization.
  • Request charity care or grant application packets.
  • Ask for the required document checklist for financial assistance.
Before day ends

6. Scan everything to PDF

This is a required close-out task. Daughter should leave with digital copies, not just a folder of paper.

  • Scan every signed page, including notary pages.
  • Name files clearly: `2026-05-19 Washington Durable Financial POA.pdf`, etc.
  • Store originals safely with mother or in a known family file.
  • Keep PDFs ready for bank, DSHS, MCO, pension, hospitals, CPA, and Medicare follow-up.

Can be done remotely after daughter leaves

These still matter, but they can usually be done from out of state once the signed documents are scanned.

Remote

Apple Health managed care plan

  • After DSHS registers the representative, call the MCO separately.
  • Ask for the plan's authorized representative or release form.
  • Submit POA and plan-specific release.
  • Use Washington HCA general line: 1-800-562-3022.
  • Review Apple Health Medicaid coverage.
Remote after signatures

Taxes and IRS access

  • Submit IRS 2848 if a CPA, enrolled agent, or attorney will represent mother.
  • Submit IRS 8821 if daughter or preparer only needs tax information access.
  • List every missing tax year separately on the forms.
  • Use IRS Get Transcript with the CPA/preparer.
  • Request missing pension 1099-Rs, then file back-year returns with CPA assistance.
Washington DOR, if applicable

Washington does not have personal income tax, so most ordinary back-year income tax work is federal IRS work. Use Washington DOR's Confidential Tax Information Authorization only if a Washington state tax, business, estate, or property tax matter applies.

Resource: Washington DOR forms and Form 42 2446.

RemoteWatch dates

Medicare

Medicare enrollment starts 3 months before the 65th birthday and ends 3 months after. Missing the window can cause penalties.

  • Check whether mother is already receiving Social Security and may be auto-enrolled in Parts A and B.
  • If not auto-enrolled, apply through SSA Medicare signup or Medicare.gov signup guidance.
  • Once on Medicare, use CMS-1696 Appointment of Representative, the CMS-1696 PDF, or a CMS-accepted conforming written instrument.
  • If mother becomes dual eligible, notify DSHS of Medicare enrollment.
Remote

Hospital billing and charity care

  • Submit signed release/proxy/billing authorization forms.
  • Request itemized bills and financial assistance applications.
  • Gather required tax records, bank statements, proof of income, and benefit letters.
  • Track every submitted packet by date, fax/mail/upload method, and confirmation number.
Remote if movingPlan early

Potential move to North Carolina

Medicaid does not transfer between states. Medicare is federal and moves with the person.

  • Notify Washington DSHS/Apple Health and request termination at month end.
  • Establish NC residency and proof of address.
  • Apply through NC ePASS or review NC Medicaid application guidance.
  • Call NC Medicaid Contact Center: 1-888-245-0179.
  • Verify current NC Medicaid income and resource rules before the move; older planning notes estimated about $20,000/year for a single adult.
  • Expect up to 45 days processing, or up to 90 days for disability applications; keep medical receipts during any gap.
  • Ask whether benefits can be retroactive to the application date and what receipts should be saved during any coverage gap.
  • Submit NC DHHS DMA-5202c authorized representative form.
  • Consider NC Durable Financial POA and NC Health Care POA/Advance Directive after residency is established.
Remote

Legal help if needed

Short scripts daughter can use

Keep calls short. The goal is to get the right form, signature rule, submission method, and next step.

Notary

"I need a notary appointment for my mother to sign several Washington POA and authorization documents. She has limited mobility. Can you come to her, what ID is required, what is the fee, and can we sign multiple documents in one visit?"

Bank

"My mother wants to add me as agent under her Power of Attorney, not as joint owner. What bank-specific POA certification or agent forms do you require, do both of us need to appear in person, and what documents should we bring?"

DSHS / Apple Health

"My mother wants me added as her authorized representative for Apple Health/Medicaid. We have DSHS Form 14-532 and a durable financial POA. How do we submit them, and how do I receive notices, renewal forms, and case information?"

Apple Health MCO

"I am being added as my mother's authorized representative through DSHS. What release or representative form does your plan require so I can discuss coverage, claims, care coordination, and notices?"

Pension / union plan

"My mother receives pension benefits and wants me authorized to help. Do you accept a Washington Durable Financial POA, or do you require your own representative form? We also need missing 1099-Rs, payment details, direct deposit confirmation, and confirmation that any divorce/QDRO order is on file."

Hospital records and billing

"My mother wants me authorized to help with medical records, billing, financial assistance, and portal access. Which release, proxy, and billing authorization forms should she sign, and how can we submit them if she has limited mobility?"

Medicare / CMS-1696

"I am helping my mother with Medicare. What is her enrollment status, what deadlines apply, and what is the current process to add me as an appointed representative using CMS-1696 or an accepted written authorization?"

Tax preparer / CPA

"My mother may have unfiled prior tax years. She can sign IRS 2848 or 8821. Which form do you need, which years should be listed, and can you request wage/income transcripts and help file back-year returns?"

NC Medicaid

"My mother may move from Washington to North Carolina. I understand Medicaid does not transfer. What proof of residency, income documents, authorized representative form, and timing should we prepare before applying through ePASS?"

Health care POA appendix

This is separate from the financial POA and only covers medical decisions when mother cannot make or communicate her own decisions.

  • Sign Washington Durable Power of Attorney for Health Care; a backup agent is recommended. Resource: End of Life Washington durable POA planning.
  • Consider Washington Advance Directive / Living Will for CPR, ventilator, artificial nutrition, and comfort-focused care preferences. Resource: End of Life Washington.
  • Use notary or two disinterested witnesses; notarization is preferred for institutional acceptance.
  • Give copies to primary care, specialists, hospital records, and daughter; ask for upload into the electronic medical record.
  • Mother should carry a wallet card noting that a health care POA exists and where it is kept.
  • If moving to NC, execute NC Health Care POA and NC Advance Directive after residency is established. Resources: NC advance directive forms and NC DHHS Supporting Choice.
