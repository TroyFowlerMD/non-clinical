Destinations

Oak Island, NC
Best overall dog-friendly beach destination · Rank #1
🚗 ~5.5–6 hrs from Asheville 📍 I-26 E → I-85 S → US-74 E → NC-87 S 🏖️ 14 miles of shoreline 🐾 America's Top 10 Dog Beaches

Beach Quality & Family Suitability

Oak Island offers 14 miles of natural, wide, uncrowded shoreline — notably less commercialized than Myrtle Beach or Wrightsville. The beach faces south, producing calmer surf ideal for young swimmers. The western end (The Point at Kings Lynn Drive) features tide pools, shelling, and occasional shark tooth discoveries. The east end near Caswell Beach offers off-leash dog access dawn–9 a.m. year-round.

🐕 Dog Beach Rules — June

✅ Named one of America's Top 10 Dog Beaches — leash permitted year-round, no hour bans.
  • Dogs allowed on beach year-round, any time of day — leash max 6 ft (Mar 16 – Oct 14)
  • No breed restrictions, no permit required; owners must clean up
  • Off-leash Oct 15 – Mar 15
  • Caswell Beach (adjacent): Leash year-round; off-leash before 9 a.m. daily
  • Pet fee: $0–$150; Better Beach Rentals waives 1st pet fee

Parking & Logistics

Free beach access points throughout. Harris Teeter and Food Lion on-island for groceries. Southport (10 min) for restaurants, shops, and Fort Fisher Ferry terminal.

💰 Rental Cost Estimate (June Peak)

Non-oceanfront 3-BR: ~$200–350/night. Oceanfront 3-BR: $400–800+/night. Budget fit: 🟢 YES — near-beach 3-BR options at/near $200–250 exist for midweek June 21–25.

Pawleys Island / Litchfield Beach, SC
Best shorter-drive option with all-day dog access · Rank #2
🚗 ~5 hr 47 min from Asheville 📍 I-26 E → US-501 E → US-17 S 🐾 Dogs on leash all day 8am–8pm

Beach Quality & Family Suitability

Pawleys Island and adjacent Litchfield Beach are known for a relaxed, "arrogantly shabby" atmosphere with wide, uncrowded beaches. Gentle surf, good shelling, dolphin sightings, and quieter vibe than Myrtle Beach (25 mi north). Excellent for families wanting a laid-back coastal experience.

🐕 Dog Beach Rules — June

✅ Dogs allowed on beach May 1–Sep 30, 8:00 a.m.–8:00 p.m. on leash
  • Leash required at all times in the water and on sand (May–Sep)
  • No pets on sand dunes; waste must be bagged
  • Dogs not allowed 10 p.m. to 1 hr before sunrise (Apr 15–Oct 31)
  • Litchfield Beach: Leash 9 a.m.–7 p.m.; off-leash voice-responsive outside those hours
  • Huntington Beach State Park: leashed dogs on south beach year-round

💰 Rental Cost Estimate (June Peak)

Non-beachfront 3-BR condo: ~$150–250/night. Beach-adjacent: $300–500+. Oceanfront: $578–768+/night. Budget fit: 🟡 PARTIAL — golf course condos in budget; beachfront exceeds $250/night.

Holden Beach, NC
Most beautiful beach · Free concert June 22 · ⚠️ Severe dog hour bans
🚗 ~6.5–7 hrs from Asheville 🏖️ 9 miles of exceptional shoreline ⚠️ Dogs banned 9am–5pm peak season

🐕 Dog Restriction Warning — June

⚠️ Dogs NOT allowed on Holden Beach 9 a.m.–5 p.m., May 20–September 10
  • Outside those hours (5 p.m.–9 a.m.) leashed dogs allowed
  • Only 4 morning hours + 3 evening hours accessible with a dog
  • Pets may not be left unattended in rentals at any time
  • Hobbs Realty "Bring Your Pooch" program: 1 dog up to 65 lbs at no extra cost

Why Consider Despite Restrictions

June 22 Free Concert: The Main Event Band, 6:30–8 p.m. at the pavilion under the Holden Beach bridge. FREE, BYO chairs. June 25 Turtle Events: Children's Turtle Time (4 p.m., ages 3–6, FREE) and Turtle Talk (7 p.m., all ages, FREE) at Holden Beach Chapel. Plus: Magic Mountain Fun Park water slides, Shallotte River Swamp Park gator tours.

Ocean Isle Beach, NC
Most budget-friendly NC coast · Most restrictive dog access in Brunswick Islands
🚗 ~6 hrs from Asheville ⚠️ No dogs 9am–6pm Memorial Day–Labor Day

🐕 Dog Restrictions — June

⚠️ No dogs on beach 9 a.m.–6 p.m., Memorial Day through Labor Day — most restrictive in Brunswick Islands
  • Only 3 early-morning + evening hours remain with a dog
  • Dogs must be on leash on island roads/public spaces when off rental property

Why Consider Despite Restrictions

Largest budget-friendly rental inventory on the NC coast. Free concerts Fridays 6:30 p.m. (June 26 in your window). OIB Summer Market Tuesdays, Scavenger Hunt Wednesdays (June 23–24 in window). Moonlight Movies evenings. Fenced-yard options available on Airbnb. Budget fit: 🟢 YES — most affordable NC beach option.

Isle of Palms / Sullivan's Island / Folly Beach, SC
Closest SC beaches · Shortest drive · NOT recommended with a dog in June
🚗 ~4 hr 15 min (shortest drive) 🚫 Near-total dog exclusion during waking hours

🐕 Dog Restrictions — June

🚫 NOT recommended with a dog in June — near-total exclusion from beach during all waking hours
  • Isle of Palms: Dogs banned 9 a.m.–7 p.m., April 1–September 14. Only 2 hrs of morning access.
  • Sullivan's Island: Dogs banned 10 a.m.–6 p.m., May 1–September 30. Visitor permit required ($50).
  • Folly Beach: Dogs banned 10 a.m.–6 p.m., May 1–September 30.

The shorter drive is entirely offset by near-total dog exclusion from the beach during waking hours. Not recommended as a primary destination if dog beach access is a priority in June.

Best budget availability · Restricted dog beach hours · Great for non-dog trips
🚗 ~5 hrs from Asheville ⚠️ No dogs 10am–5pm (May 1–Labor Day) 💰 Condos from $150+/night

⚠️ Dogs restricted during peak beach hours throughout Grand Strand
  • Myrtle Beach: No dogs 10 a.m.–5 p.m., May 1–Labor Day. Dog parks available.

Why Consider

Widest budget rental inventory. Enormous activities: boardwalk, SkyWheel, Broadway at the Beach, go-karts, water parks. Dog parks as beach alternatives. Recommended only as a cost-saving measure if dog beach access is not the priority.
